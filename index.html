Texas Retired Teachers Foundation

We are a non-profit organization devoted to serving Texas' public educators of the past, present, and future.

Our History

The Texas Retired Teachers Foundation (TRTF) was formed in 1988 as a part of the Texas Retired Teachers Association (TRTA) under the name the Building Fund Trust. The trust was formed for the purpose of acquiring and providing a home office building for TRTA in Austin.

From 1988 to 2000, the Building Fund Trust raised funds through the “Walkway by the Wall” campaign, accumulating as much as $125,000 in one year. The Building Fund Trust also purchased a lot at 12th and Trinity streets in Austin in 1992, which was rented out for parking until construction started.

In 2000, TRTF was incorporated as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit foundation with an educational and charitable mission. Such nonprofit foundations are prohibited from participating in lobbying and other political activities.

"A Helping Hand" Program

Since the program's inception in 2010, we have awarded over $70,000 in grants for medical, dental, and utility bills.

Launched in 2010, "A Helping Hand" provides public education retirees with charitable assistance for a one-time special need or other short-term hardship. Recipients include anyone who is receiving or is eligible to receive an annuity from the Teacher Retirement System of Texas (TRS).

Student Scholarships

Since the program's inception in 2008, TRTF has awarded over $45,000 in scholarship funds.

We award scholarships to Texas college students pursuing a bachelor's or master's degree in Education. Student scholarship applicants must be a relative of a member of the Texas Retired Teachers Association (TRTA) and should include their official high school or college transcripts with their application.

Classroom Assistance Grants

Since the program's inception in 2008, TRTF has given away over $41,000 in classroom assistance grants.

Classroom Assistance Grants are awarded to public school teachers who on a daily basis demonstrate commitment to public education and Texas children through the creation of programs, events, or other learning platforms.
